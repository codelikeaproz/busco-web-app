BUSCO Recognized for Quality Milling Standards

BUSCO Sugar Milling Co., Inc. has been formally recognized by the Philippine Sugar Regulatory Administration (SRA) and industry peers for its outstanding performance in quality milling operations, reaffirming the company's position as one of Mindanao's most reliable and forward-looking sugar mills.

The recognition was awarded during the Regional Sugar Industry Summit held in Cagayan de Oro City, gathering mill operators, planters' associations, and government representatives from across Mindanao. BUSCO was among a select group of milling companies honored for consistently meeting and exceeding technical quality benchmarks over the past milling season.

Company officials, led by the Mill Manager and department heads from engineering, quality control, and operations, accepted the award on behalf of the BUSCO workforce ‚Äî a team of over 600 employees and thousands of partner planters across Quezon, Bukidnon.

What the Recognition Covers

The award specifically highlights BUSCO's performance across three key evaluation areas: juice extraction efficiency, sugar color and purity metrics, and mill time utilization rates. These indicators are tracked throughout the milling season and benchmarked against regional and national standards set by the SRA.

BUSCO's quality control laboratory, operating around the clock during milling season, plays a central role in this achievement. Technicians conduct hourly analyses of cane juice, massecuite, and final sugar product ‚Äî ensuring that each batch of raw sugar meets both domestic distribution and export-grade requirements.

Key Achievements This Season
Achieved 98.6% mill availability ‚Äî exceeding the industry average by 4.2 percentage points during peak milling weeks.
Maintained sugar color ratings below 1,200 ICUMSA units, meeting international standards for raw sugar quality.
Improved extraction efficiency by 2.1% year-on-year through optimized tandem settings and preventive maintenance protocols.
Zero critical safety incidents during the 2025‚Äì2026 milling season, reflecting strong operational discipline and worker training programs.

A Culture of Continuous Improvement

For BUSCO's engineering and operations leadership, the award is not an endpoint ‚Äî but a milestone in a longer journey of continuous improvement. The mill has invested significantly in equipment upgrades over the past three years, including the modernization of its evaporator station, installation of automated monitoring systems in the boiler house, and expansion of its laboratory instrumentation.

"This recognition belongs to every member of the BUSCO family ‚Äî from the operators on the mill floor to our partner planters in the field. Quality is a shared commitment."

The company's Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) program, launched in early 2024, is widely credited for the improved mill availability scores. Under TPM, all departments participate in equipment care, early fault detection, and systematic root-cause analysis of downtime events ‚Äî a cultural shift that has paid dividends in operational reliability.

Strengthening Planter Partnerships

Beyond milling operations, the award also recognizes BUSCO's expanded support programs for its partner planters. The company's Agronomic Services Division conducted over 80 farm visits during the off-milling season in 2025, providing technical guidance on variety selection, fertilization schedules, and crop protection strategies.

These efforts directly translate into better cane quality at harvest ‚Äî higher sucrose content, lower fiber, and reduced impurity loads ‚Äî which contribute positively to mill performance metrics and ultimately benefit both the company and its planter-partners through improved sugar yield per ton of cane.

Looking Ahead to the 2026‚Äì2027 Season

BUSCO's management has announced plans to further invest in mill infrastructure ahead of the incoming season, with targeted upgrades to the crystallization station and additional capacity in the raw sugar warehouse. New planter incentive programs tied to cane quality are also under development, expected to be rolled out before the next crop cycle begins.

With this recognition in hand and a clear roadmap for improvement, BUSCO Sugar Milling Co., Inc. enters the second half of 2026 with renewed momentum ‚Äî committed to serving its planters, its workforce, and the wider Bukidnon community with the highest standards of sugar milling excellence.
