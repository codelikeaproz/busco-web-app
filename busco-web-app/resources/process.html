Cane Delivery & Weighing

The process begins with the arrival of harvested sugarcane from local farms. Trucks pass through our precision weighbridges to record the gross weight, ensuring accurate compensation for our partner farmers before proceeding to the unloading area.

  • check_circleAutomated weighbridge system
  • check_circleQuality sampling upon arrival
02

Cane Cutting & Shredding

To facilitate efficient juice extraction, the cane stalks are prepared by heavy-duty knives and shredders. This mechanical process ruptures the cane cells, turning the stalks into a fibrous mass ready for milling.

  • check_circleHigh-speed rotary knives
  • check_circleFiber preparation index monitoring
03
water_drop

Juice Extraction

The shredded cane passes through a series of heavy rollers in the mill tandem. Under immense pressure, the sugar-rich juice is squeezed out from the fiber. The remaining fiber, called bagasse, is used as eco-friendly fuel for our boilers.

  • check_circleMulti-stage milling tandem
  • check_circleImbibition water application
04
science

Clarification

The raw juice contains impurities like soil and fiber particles. In the clarification stage, the juice is heated and treated with lime to neutralize acidity and settle out impurities, resulting in a clear, golden liquid known as clarified juice.

  • check_circleLiming and heating process
  • check_circleSedimentation tanks
05
mode_heat

Evaporation

The clarified juice, which is about 85% water, is pumped into multiple-effect evaporators. Here, water is boiled off under vacuum conditions to concentrate the juice into a thick, sweet syrup without caramelizing the sugar.

  • check_circleMultiple-effect evaporator vessels
  • check_circleEnergy-efficient steam usage
06
grain

Crystallization

The syrup is further concentrated in vacuum pans until it becomes supersaturated. At this critical point, "seed" crystals are introduced, causing sugar crystals to grow. The result is "massecuite," a mixture of sugar crystals and molasses.

  • check_circleVacuum pan boiling
  • check_circleControlled crystal growth
07
rotate_right

Centrifugation

The massecuite is fed into high-speed centrifugal machines. These baskets spin at high velocity, separating the sugar crystals from the molasses through perforated screens. The result is raw sugar.

  • check_circleHigh-speed basket centrifugals
  • check_circleMolasses separation
08
inventory_2

Drying & Bagging

Finally, the raw sugar is dried to remove residual moisture, preventing caking during storage. It is then weighed and packaged into sacks for distribution or bulk shipment to refineries and markets.

  • check_circleHot air rotary dryers
  • check_circleAutomated weighing & stitching
